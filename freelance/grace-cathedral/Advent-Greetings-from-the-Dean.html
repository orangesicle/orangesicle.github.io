8 December 2016 
Dear Brothers and Sisters in Christ,
 
What a blessing it is to be traveling together through this Advent season!
 
I could not be more happy with our "Coming Home for Advent and Christmas" preaching series at the 11 a.m. service. So far we have heard the former Presiding Bishop Katherine Jefferts Schori, our own Dean Emeritus Alan Jones, and former Cathedral Canon and current Church Divinity School of the Pacific Professor Susanna Singer.
 
Now we are looking forward to the former Cathedral School Head Malcolm Manson, Ellen Clark King, Bishop Marc Andrus and former Bishop William Swing. These are some of the best preachers of our time each reflecting on what it means to find your spiritual home.
 
The chapter house has been thrilled that our new Executive Pastor Ellen Clark King arrives for her first day on Sunday December 11. She preaches her first sermon on Sunday, December 18 at 11 a.m. We are very pleased to welcome her and her husband the Rev. Jeremy Clark-King to our community.
 
This afternoon I spoke with Benjamin Bergery and Jim Campbell our 2016 Artists in Residence as they installed their magnificent 47-foot-high light sculpture in the north aisle of the cathedral. We will be blessing them and their work at a special Evensong at 5:15 p.m. tonight.
 
I'll also be talking with them at The Forum this Sunday, December 11 at 9:30 a.m. to learn more about their important work. Later that day at 5 p.m., we will have the official opening reception.
 
There are so many great ways to get involved in service this season at the cathedral. We have donated and wrapped 200 presents for children of incarcerated parents. Our yoga community is collecting and wrapping presents for others in need while we are working with Cathedral School for Boys to prepare food and packed toiletries. You can volunteer to usher at a concert. Let us know what you might be able to do to help.
 
May God bless you with the presence of the holy during this season of darkness and light. Thank you for being the light of the world!
 
Love,
Malcolm

We are so grateful to our warm and loving congregation during this season of stewardship. Today, we are 82% to our goal of 400 pledging households participating for 2017. Hundreds of hearts representing pledges adorn our St. Francis statue. We also thank our stewardship committee for their 100% participation and leadership. During the month of December, we are publishing the name of each pledging family as a welcome to thousands of Christmas visitors to Grace Cathedral.

If you call Grace your home, please renew your support or join us with your pledge.

Please join us in unveiling a new light art installation by our 2016 Artists in Residence Benjamin Bergery and Jim Campbell. Jacob's Dream: A Luminous Path evokes a connection between earth and heaven, between the seen and unseen. All are welcome at several great events planned in celebration of the opening. First, tonight's Evensong service at 5:15 p.m. will include a special procession and blessing of the artwork.
On Sunday, the artists will share the inspiration and creative process for Jacob's Dream at The Forum at 9:30 a.m. The official opening is later that evening at 5 p.m., with a reception to follow. The piece is on view daily during normal cathedral hours.
 

The Ven Malcolm H. Manson, Archdeacon for Schools and former headmaster of The Cathedral School for Boys, will preach at the 11 a.m service this Sunday, December 11
 as part of our "Coming Home for Advent and Christmas" sermon series.





The Advent Sale continues this Sunday, December 11, right after the 11 a.m. service. This holiday fair features handcrafted goods made by cathedral families and friends. While shopping at the Advent Sale, enjoy coffee hour hosted by The Men of Grace after the 11 a.m. service. This is a great opportunity to learn more about this ministry.




The Sunday 4:30 Book Study Group is reading a special text for Christmas entitled Advent with Evelyn Underhill, edited by Christopher Webber. The book features selections from the writings of the woman who reintroduced Christian mysticism to the 20th century, with suggestions for prayer and meditation for each day in Advent through Epiphany. The group will meet on Sundays until January 18 (excluding Christmas Day) to discuss the prior week's readings and share reading experiences. For more information, email the group organizer.
